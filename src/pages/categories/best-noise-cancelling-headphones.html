Understanding ANC Technology

How ANC Works

Active Noise Cancellation uses microphones to detect ambient noise and creates inverse sound waves to cancel it out, effectively reducing unwanted sounds.

Types of ANC

Feedforward ANC uses external microphones, feedback ANC uses internal microphones, and hybrid ANC combines both for maximum effectiveness.

What ANC Can't Block

ANC is most effective against low-frequency sounds like engine noise. High-frequency sounds like voices and sudden noises are harder to cancel.

Transparency Mode

Many ANC headphones feature transparency mode that lets you hear your surroundings without removing the headphones, perfect for safety and awareness.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ANC bad for your ears?

No, ANC is not harmful to your ears. In fact, it can be beneficial by allowing you to listen at lower volumes since you don't need to compete with background noise.

Can ANC cause dizziness?

Some people may experience slight dizziness when first using ANC headphones, but this usually subsides as your brain adjusts to the technology.

Do ANC headphones affect sound quality?

Modern ANC headphones maintain excellent sound quality. Some may have slight frequency response changes, but the overall audio experience is typically superior.
